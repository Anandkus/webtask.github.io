Feni is an alcoholic spirit that is produced only in Goa, India. It is exported to different parts of the world, including the US. The main types of Feni are made from either coconut sap or cashew apples, and each bottle has an approximately 43-45% alcohol content. There are many ways to enjoy this liquor, such as with ice, neat, with soft drink mixers, or in cocktails. You can also taste Feni at many bars and restaurants in Goa, or at a limited number of cocktail bars in Chicago.

Feni is a popular drink to enjoy with South Indian cuisine, which involves many dishes that are based around lentils, rice, and stews. Common dishes to enjoy with Feni are dosa (a crêpe made from lentils and rice), saaru (a tomato, lentil, and tamarind soup), and huli (spicy vegetable and lentil stew

1 Use a highball or cocktail glass to drink Feni if it is mixed. Choose a highball glass that is 8–12 fl oz (240–350 mL) in volume. A highball glass is large enough to hold Feni combined with other beverages such as Limca, lemonade, or cola.[7] A cocktail glass which has a long stem and triangular-shaped bowl is the perfect size to enjoy Feni cocktails. Cocktail glasses are typically 4–12 fl oz (120–350 mL) in volume. These glasses work best for Feni mixed with ice, lime juice, sugar, or chili.[8]

2 Drink Feni mixed with Limca for a traditional Goan cocktail. Mix together 1.5 fl oz (44 mL) of Feni and 6 fl oz (180 mL) of Limca in a highball glass. Add ice cubes and a lemon twist on the side of the glass.[9] Limca is a carbonated lemon and lime flavored drink that is sold in India. If it isn’t available in your country, try a different brand of lemon and lime soft drink. This will give a similar taste. To make a lemon twist, slice the lemon in half crosswise. The make a second, parallel cut that is 0.25 in (0.64 cm) from the edge of the first cut, creating a thin slice. Cut the pulp away from the peel, and twist the peel into a curl.[10]

3 Mix Feni with a soft drink for a simple, refreshing drink. Mix Feni with cola, lemonade, or other fruity flavors of soda. Try out different combinations to see what you prefer.[11] Drinking Feni with a mixer is a good way to familiarise yourself with the strong taste.

4 Use a green chili in your Feni cocktail to make a Firefly Fire. Use a cocktail shaker to combine 60 mL (2.0 fl oz) of cashew apple Feni with 5 mL (0.17 fl oz) of lime juice and ice. Pour the mixture over 1 green chili in a cocktail glass.[12] After pouring the cocktail, sprinkle 1 tsp (4.17 g) of brown sugar over the top.

5 Mix together coconut Feni, lime juice, and Brindao to make a Tambdé Rosa. Combine 60 mL (2.0 fl oz) of coconut Feni with 10 mL (0.34 fl oz) of Brindao, and 2 mL (0.068 fl oz) of lime juice. Pour the mixture over ice in a cocktail glass, and add a garnish of mint leaves.[13] Brindao is the extract of Kokum, which is type of fruit found in Goa.

6 Take a Cazulo Capitao to try a classic Feni cocktail. Shake 60 mL (2.0 fl oz) of cashew apple or coconut Feni with 10 mL (0.34 fl oz) of lime juice and 2 tsp (8.34 g) of white sugar in a cocktail shaker. Serve the cocktail chilled over ice.[14]
